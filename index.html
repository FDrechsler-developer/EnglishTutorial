Once inside, let's verify the first villain:
*Misaligned Chassis or Frame.

With small square verify each corner. The square must to touch both surfaces to assure 90 degrees.

Even de upper corners must to be checked.
This verification is the most important. All parts in machine are attached to frame or chassis, one misalignment means all parts misaligned, that means mess.
Here, all corners are ok!

Next dangerous villain:
*Loose
After misaligned Chassis, loose is the worse villain and easier to eliminate.

With your hand, try to move up and down the axis X carriage, if you feel loose, means that Axis Z is loose.
Axis Z loose doesn't allow to move parallel X carriage to print bed.

To solve this, tight all eccentric nuts to adjust Z vslots.
Vslots are simple parts with bearings that running within chassis profiles. To tighten you must to move the tool in clockwise and to release counter-clockwise.

With you hand try to move clockwise and counter-clockwise the hotend carriage.
If It's loose, means that 3D printing looks like shaking, loosing precision and details.

Tight the eccentric nuts in vslots to adjust hot end carriage.

With your hand move slowly up and down and clockwise. If you feel loose, means the bed print carriage is not pushing and pulling the right way the axis Y

Under bed print there are two vlost, you must to adjust the eccentric nuts the same way that you done in axis Z or hotend carriage.

Another dangerous villain:
*Dust.
If you have an open 3D printer, beware with this silent evil.

Every time that you clean up your house, lots of dust are spread through the air.
This dust settles in you furniture, in you 3D printer and your 3D print filament.
Before each use, clean all possible parts of 3D print with a soft brush, removing all visible dust.
For 3d print filament, there are lot of models of filters available in the internet to print. That certainly is a good update for your machine.

This is not a villain, but after some time can become one:
*The Nozzle.
If you print lots of pieces in a month, its recommendable to change the nozzle every month. This cheap part will keep you away from a good headache.

WARNING:
To change nozzle you must to heat the hot end to a temperature between 195-230Â°C. So, use right tool for this job, don't try to touch the heated nozzle with your bare hands.
There are lots of videos on youtube teaching how to proceed this change for each trademark.

Two more tips to finish this tutorial:
*Moisture
Moisture affects 3d print filament, creating bubles, stringing and weak printed parts.
After use always keep the filament inside boxes with silica gel and well sealed.
Open new filaments only if you are going to use them, they are hermetically vacuum sealed to prevent moisture and ensure durability

*Speed
Beginners have a wrong idea about that: fast speed doesn't means good job. It only applies to industrial machines of thousants of dollars, not to entrance and hobbist models.
Speed inserts extra vibration, increase printing temperature e flow of material. And only works with several tests. There are no recipe. So be gentle and pacient with your machines
